AJ Domingo

──────────────────────────────

Birthed in 2002, AJ Domingo was born and raised in a cluster of small suburban towns in California, better known as Santa Maria. While living in Santa Maria, AJ was involved in a plethora of community projects, ranging from being his county's respresentative for the California Environmental Justice Coalition, fundraising over $7000 for the Adopt-A-Family (AAF) Foundation, and being the project mananger for the Santa Maria School District Mental Health Initiative.

Currently, AJ is studying Business Administration with an emphasis in Marketing in Los Angeles, CA, at the University of Southern California Marshall School of Business along and Annenberg School of Communications and Journalism.

With one of his recent roles as a research assistant for the Annenberg Inclusion Initiative, AJ has been able to be part of the Academy Award Oscar's Project as well as the Inclusion Lists porject which have been published on Good Morning America and Variety. As a media and entertainment research assistant for the Annenberg Inclusion Initiative, AJ's role involves supporting the research efforts of the organization, including data collection, analysis, and report writing. AJ works closely with the research team to examine patterns of representation and diversity in media content, contribute to groundbreaking studies, and help shape the narrative around inclusive practices in the media and entertainment industry.

Outside of academic studies, AJ is very passionate about uplifting marginalized communtiies, involving himself with a non-profit organization named "Ms. Rodgers' Hood," to help provide food distribution, clothing donations, and community outreach to the South Central Community in Los Angeles. AJ has also established First Gen 4 College which provides academic consulting to high school students in his hometown.
